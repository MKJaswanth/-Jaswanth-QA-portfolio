Manual QA proof

Case studies built from real QA artifacts.

Each example is framed around product risk, testing approach, and defect evidence that helps teams make safer release decisions.

B2B Commerce Platform

A wholesale marketplace where buyer trust depends on clear navigation and protected commerce flows. Testing exposed cart access without login, broken product paths, non-clickable contact actions, and CTA routing mistakes.

Outcome: 22 cases, 19 defects, conversion and access-control risks surfaced.
Wholesale Marketplace

An e-commerce marketplace with category, hero, requirement-posting, and registration flows. QA focused on the buyer journey from landing page to lead capture and account creation.

Outcome: 13 E2E checks with navigation and validation issues documented.
Workflow Management App

A Scrum/Kanban productivity app where teams rely on dashboard accuracy, task creation, and board state visibility. Testing covered login, widgets, board columns, filters, and task workflows.

Outcome: 95 workflow cases, including header/navigation and task-flow defects.
Validation Suite

Security signal

Unsafe input handling deserved escalation.

During negative testing on search and filter behavior, I validated that script-like input was not being safely handled. I treated it as an OWASP A03:2021 Injection risk, documented the expected sanitization behavior, and recommended server-side validation plus output encoding.

Risk

Script-like input was accepted in a user-facing field.

Expected

Input should be escaped, sanitized, and rendered as harmless text.

Impact

Injection-class behavior can damage trust, data safety, and release confidence.

Recommendation

Validate server-side, encode output, and add regression coverage for unsafe payloads.

B2B Commerce Platform deep dive
Scope

Covered homepage, navigation, contact actions, cart access, product paths, CTAs, and buyer journey flows for a wholesale commerce experience.

Approach

Prioritized release-risk scenarios first: access control, conversion paths, broken links, form validation, and expected-versus-actual behavior.

What I Found

Cart access without login, incorrect CTA routing, product path failures, and static contact elements that should have opened phone or email actions.

Business Impact

Clear defect evidence helped separate cosmetic issues from risks that affect buyer trust, lead conversion, and release readiness.
